Measurement has become fundamental to the operation of networks and at-scale services---whether for management, security, diagnostics, optimization, or simply enhancing our collective understanding of the Internet as a complex system. Further, measurements are useful across points of view---from end hosts to enterprise networks and data centers to the wide area Internet. We observe that many measurements are decoupled from the protocols and applications they are designed to illuminate. The PTO currently holds data from measurement campaigns that have been performed by or in collaboration with the authors, using a small set of diverse tools such as tracebox (http:// www.tracebox.org/ ), and PATHspider (https://pathspider.net). The earliest such measurement campaign dates back to 2014, and studies connectivity risk of TCP’s Explicit Congestion Notification (ECN) extension when used as client default. PATHspider developed from the tool used in that study into a general-purpose A/B testing tool for path transparency, and has been co- developed with the PTO. It annotates its raw measurement data directly with condition information as used in the PTO data model, greatly simplifying the task of raw data analysis and conversion into observations for publication in the PTO. In addition, PATHspider provides a facility to directly upload data to the PTO as it is generated, greatly simplifying automated measurements. Our end goal is to expand each measurement we run into a longitudinal study with little effort, make up-to date data directly accessible over the PTO, and thereby increase the reach of the insights we can draw from our data.

The measurement data collected in the observatory forms the basis for quantifying impairments on path transparency when traffic is altered or treated differently on a network path based on the protocol stack in use. This makes the data essential for future data-driven decisions in protocol engineering and an can provide an incentive for operational correction of these impairments.
